What We Do

Our Mission

Connecting Law Students with Youth

Law-Tie bridges the gap between aspiring legal professionals and young people facing court injustices. We generate structured, verifiable case reports that serve as professional portfolio evidence — creating a win-win ecosystem where advocacy meets opportunity.

01

The Problem with Pro Bono

Attorneys at large firms are typically expected to devote only ~1% of their time to pro bono work — making it a shield against social accountability rather than a viable model for justice. Training is minimized, resources are rationed, and paying clients always come first.

02

A Better Model

LawTie pairs pro-bono lawyers who genuinely want to help with law students and passionate individuals who hold verifiable, evidence-based legal knowledge. Students complete lawyer-supervised tasks — bridging commitment with capacity.

03

More Than Legal Help

Because law students are closer in age to the youth we serve (under 25), they bring not only legal support but also emotional understanding and peer-level advice — something traditional legal aid rarely provides.

04

Building Careers

Law students earn an official LawTie record — shareable on LinkedIn or submitted to employers — documenting their real-world impact. We also offer free certified training courses for both students and underrepresented youth.

Our mission is to help youth under 25 who lack the knowledge, resources, or authority to advocate for themselves — connecting them with pro-bono lawyers, sided by early-career professionals driven by purpose. Whether facing discrimination in courts because of age, race, or gender, every case submitted to LawTie is reviewed with the utmost care and matched with the most suitable lawyer and law student.
